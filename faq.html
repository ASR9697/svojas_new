Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We have answers. Find quick answers to some of the most common questions we receive about our services and the shipping process. If you don't find your answer here, please don't hesitate to contact us directly.

Quoting & Booking

What information do I need to get an accurate freight quote?+

To give you the most accurate quote, our smart form will guide you. Generally, we need your contact info, the origin/destination, and details about the freight like its type, weight, and dimensions.

How long is my quote valid for?+

Market rates for freight can change quickly. Typically, our quotes are valid for 7 days, but this can vary. Your quote will specify its expiration date.

Services & Capabilities

What is the difference between FTL and LTL?+

FTL (Full Truckload) means your freight is the only shipment on a dedicated truck. LTL (Less-than-Truckload) means your freight shares space on a truck with other shipments, which is a cost-effective option for smaller loads.

What areas do you service in the USA?+

We provide logistics solutions across all 48 contiguous United States.

Pricing, Billing & Insurance

Does my freight quote include insurance?+

By law, all carriers have a minimum level of liability coverage, but it is limited and may not cover the full value of your goods. We strongly recommend purchasing separate freight insurance for full-value protection, which we can help you arrange.

What are your standard payment terms?+

Our standard payment terms are Net 30 for approved clients. We accept various payment methods, including ACH, wire transfer, and company checks. If you have different requirements, please discuss them with our team.

Shipping Operations

What is a Bill of Lading (BOL)?+

The Bill of Lading (BOL) is the most important document in shipping. It acts as a receipt, a contract, and a document of title. A BOL is required for every shipment and must be given to the driver upon pickup.

What should I do if my shipment arrives damaged?+

In the rare event of damage, it is critical to note it on the delivery receipt before signing it. Take clear photos of the damage and contact your Svojas Inc. representative immediately. We will guide you through the claims process.
