Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about shipping, logistics, and working with Svojas Inc.

Quoting & Booking

What information do I need to get an accurate freight quote? +

To provide the most accurate rate and avoid future adjustments, please have the following ready:

  • Origin & Destination Zip Codes: Precise locations help us calculate mileage and routing.
  • Freight Description: Commodity type, handling units (pallets, crates), and dimensions (L x W x H).
  • Total Weight: Accurate weight is crucial for safety and compliance.
  • Accessorials: Do you need a liftgate? Is it a residential delivery? Is an appointment required?
How long is my quote valid for? +
Due to the dynamic nature of the freight market (fuel costs, capacity, seasonality), our spot market quotes are typically valid for 7 days. However, if you have regular shipping volumes, we can discuss long-term contract rates to provide price stability. Your specific quote document will always list its expiration date.
Can I book a shipment for same-day pickup? +
Yes, we often accommodate same-day pickups! However, availability depends on truck capacity in your specific area and the time the request is made. For the best chance of securing a truck, please submit your booking request as early in the day as possible (ideally before 12:00 PM local time).

Services & Capabilities

What is the difference between FTL and LTL? +

FTL (Full Truckload)

You rent the entire trailer. Your freight is the only cargo on board and goes directly to the destination. It's faster, has less handling risk, but costs more if the truck isn't full.

LTL (Less-than-Truckload)

You pay only for the space you use. Your freight shares the truck with other shippers' cargo. It stops at terminals along the way, making it more cost-effective but slightly slower.

What areas do you service? +
We provide comprehensive logistics solutions across all 48 contiguous United States. Whether you are shipping coast-to-coast or just across state lines, we have the network to handle it. We can also facilitate cross-border shipments to Canada and Mexico upon request.
Do you handle hazardous materials (Hazmat)? +
Yes, we work with a network of certified Hazmat carriers. When requesting a quote, please mark the "Hazmat" option and be prepared to provide the UN number and Class of the material so we can match you with a properly licensed driver.

Pricing & Insurance

Does my freight quote include insurance? +

Standard quotes include the carrier's basic limited liability coverage. This is often calculated by weight (e.g., $0.50 per lb) and is rarely enough to cover the full value of expensive goods.

Recommendation: We strongly advise purchasing All-Risk Cargo Insurance for valuable shipments. We can add this to your quote for a small fee, giving you full value protection against loss or damage.

What are your payment terms? +
For established business clients with approved credit, our standard terms are Net 30 days. We accept payments via ACH, Wire Transfer, and Company Check. For first-time shippers or individuals, we may require prepayment via credit card or wire transfer before the shipment is dispatched.

Operations & Documentation

What is a Bill of Lading (BOL)? +
The Bill of Lading (BOL) is the "ticket" for your freight. It serves three purposes:
  1. Receipt: Proof that the carrier picked up the goods.
  2. Contract: Legal agreement to transport the goods.
  3. Title: Document of ownership.

Important: You must give the driver the specific BOL we provide at pickup to ensure proper billing and tracking.

What should I do if my shipment arrives damaged? +
In the unfortunate event of damage, follow these steps immediately:
  1. Inspect thoroughly: Check all sides of the pallet/crate while the driver is still there.
  2. Notate the POD: Write specific details of the damage on the Proof of Delivery receipt before signing. Do not just sign "Subject to Inspection."
  3. Take Pictures: Photograph the damage from multiple angles.
  4. Do NOT refuse: Unless the shipment is totally destroyed, accept it and mitigate the loss. Refusing can complicate the claim.
  5. Contact Us: Notify Svojas Inc. immediately so we can begin the claims process.
