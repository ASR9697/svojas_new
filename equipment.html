Equipment Guide

Choosing the right equipment is crucial for a successful shipment. This guide provides an overview of the common trailer and container types we use to transport your freight safely and efficiently.

Dry Van Trailer

The most common type of trailer, a dry van is a fully enclosed box used to protect freight from the elements. They are ideal for moving pallets, boxes, and most forms of consumer goods.

  • Typical Length: 53 feet
  • Max Freight Weight: ~45,000 lbs
  • Dimensions: 102" W x 110" H
  • Common Uses: General dry freight, consumer packaged goods, electronics.

Refrigerated Trailer (Reefer)

Similar to a dry van but with a temperature-control unit, reefers are used to transport perishable goods that require a specific temperature range, from frozen to heated.

  • Typical Length: 53 feet
  • Max Freight Weight: ~42,500 lbs
  • Dimensions: 98" W x 102" H
  • Common Uses: Produce, frozen foods, pharmaceuticals, floral.

Flatbed Trailer

A flatbed is an open-deck trailer with no sides or roof, designed for cargo that is oversized, bulky, or needs to be loaded by crane. Freight must be properly secured and tarped for protection.

  • Typical Length: 48 or 53 feet
  • Max Freight Weight: ~48,000 lbs
  • Dimensions: 102" W x 60" H Deck
  • Common Uses: Construction materials, heavy machinery, large equipment.

Step-Deck Trailer

Also known as a drop-deck, this trailer has both an upper and lower deck, allowing for the transport of taller items (up to 10' high) without the need for special permits.

  • Typical Length: 11' upper deck + 42' lower deck
  • Max Freight Weight: ~48,000 lbs
  • Dimensions: 102" W x 120" H Max Freight
  • Common Uses: Taller equipment, tractors, machinery that can be driven on.

Removable Gooseneck (RGN)

For the tallest and heaviest loads, an RGN is the ideal solution. The front of the trailer detaches, creating a ramp that allows heavy, drivable machinery to be loaded easily from the ground.

  • Typical Well Length: 29 feet
  • Max Freight Weight: 42,000 lbs to 150,000+ lbs
  • Dimensions: 102" W x 140" H Max Freight
  • Common Uses: Excavators, agricultural combines, large industrial equipment.

Intermodal Containers

The backbone of global trade, these standardized steel boxes are designed to be efficiently transferred between ships, trains, and trucks without the need to unload the cargo. This versatility is key for international and long-distance domestic shipping.

Common Container Types:

Standard Dry Container

The most common container, used for a wide variety of general cargo.

Common Sizes: 20' GP, 40' GP

High Cube (HC) Container

Provides an extra foot of height, ideal for taller or high-volume cargo.

Common Sizes: 40' HC, 45' HC

Refrigerated (Reefer) Container

Insulated and equipped with a refrigeration unit for temperature-sensitive goods.

Common Sizes: 20' RF, 40' RF

Open Top Container

Features a removable tarpaulin roof for cargo that needs to be loaded from the top by crane.

Common Sizes: 20' OT, 40' OT

Flat Rack Container

Has collapsible or no sides, designed for heavy, wide, or oversized cargo.

Common Sizes: 20' FR, 40' FR
